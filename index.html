Hi, my name is Pol. I am a PhD student in Computational and Applied Physics at UPC under the supervision of Professor Claudio Cazorla and Professor Edgardo Saucedo. My research primarily focuses on computational condensed matter physics, where I investigate novel families of semiconductor materials expected to exhibit favorable thermal and optoelectronic properties for energy materials applications. Some potential applications include photovoltaics, optoelectronic devices, and solid-state batteries, among others.
In addition to my work in materials science, I have a keen interest in the application of physics to emerging technologies, in computer science, in machine learning applications, and in data analysis.
I am also an assistant professor at UPC, where I teach physics to first-year engineering students.
