About

I am a PhD student in Computational Physics at UPC, working under the supervision of Prof. Claudio Cazorla. My research focuses on computational condensed matter physics, utilizing first-principles methods such as Density Functional Theory (DFT), Molecular Dynamics simulations, and Machine Learning techniques. Among other topics, I study how anharmonic phonon modes influence the optoelectronic properties of crystalline solid-state systems. This research helps improve our understanding of fundamental science while also contributing to the development of novel materials for next-generation energy and optoelectronic technologies. Additionally, I work as a Teaching Assistant, teaching physics to first-year engineering students.
